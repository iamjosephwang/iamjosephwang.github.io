2.What is machine learning?

Field of study that gives computers the ability to learn without being explicitly programmed. — Arthur Samuel

3.two main types of machine learning

3.1 Supervised learning

Supervised learning expects you to give your learning algorithm examples of ‘input to output’ mappings(series of input x and correct answer y)

We will first train our model by using ‘input x and correct label y’ mappings.
After our model learns for those (x, y) pairs, we expect it to give a reasonably accurate prediction or guess of the output, that is to give a brand new input x, our model will produce appropriate corresponding output y

can find more details on Wikipedia: Supervised learning

two major types of supervised learning

  1. In regression problems:
    we’re expected to let your lines(your algorithms) fit your data.
    To define a little bit more terminology, we’re expected to predict a number from infinitely many possible numbers.

  2. In classification problems:
    In classification cases, we’re expected to predict categories. the categories can be nonnumeric.
    we’re expected to use lines to find some boundary that separates out different categories.(in the breast cancer case, for instance,separates out the malignant tumors from benign ones)
    So our learning algorithm has to decide how to fit a boundary line through this data

Note: In Classification cases, the term ‘class’ and ‘category’ ,when referring to output, are often used interchangeably.
So, we need to:
know how to choose the most appropriate lines(curves) for our complex data(a stright line,or maybe a more complicated curve)

3.2 Unsupervised Learning

We’re expected to find something interesting or what patterns or structures that might be in this unlabeled data

  1. clustering - group similar data together
    clustering algorithm: places unlabeled data into different clusters.

  2. Anomaly detection - detect unusual events
    This turns out to be really important for fraud detection in the financial system, where unusual events and transactions could be signs of fraud.

  3. Dimensionality reduction - compress data using fewer numbers
