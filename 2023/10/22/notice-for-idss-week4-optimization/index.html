3. hard constraints vs. soft constraints

  1. hard constraints: use an optimisation algorithm that supports hard constraints inherently. the constraints will be specified as a convex or rectangular region of the space.
    Pros and Cons.

  2. soft constraints: apply penalties to objective function to discourage solutions that violate the constraints.
    egregiously:in an outstandingly, bad way
    Pros and Cons.

  3. Relaxation of objective functions
    It can be hard to solve discrete optimisation and constrained optimisation problems efficiently. algorithms try to find similar continuous or unconstrained optimisation problems and solve instead.That is, relaxation.
